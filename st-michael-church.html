St. Michael the Archangel Church

A magnificent Spanish colonial church built in the 16th century, showcasing beautiful architecture and rich religious heritage.

About St. Michael the Archangel Church

St. Michael the Archangel Church stands as one of the oldest and most significant religious landmarks in Argao, Cebu. Built during the Spanish colonial period in the 16th century, this historic church represents centuries of faith, devotion, and architectural excellence that continues to inspire visitors from around the world.

The church features stunning Spanish colonial architecture with intricate stone work, beautiful stained glass windows, and ornate religious artwork. Inside, visitors can admire the beautiful altar, religious paintings, and statues that date back hundreds of years.

Operating Hours

Daily: 5:00 AM - 8:00 PM

Mass Schedule: Weekdays (5:30 AM, 6:00 PM) | Sundays (5:30 AM, 7:00 AM, 6:00 PM)

How to Get There

From Cebu City: Take a bus to Argao town center (2-3 hours). The church is located in the heart of Argao town, just a short walk from the bus terminal.

By Private Vehicle: Drive south from Cebu City via the national highway. The church is easily visible in Argao town center with nearby parking available.

Tips: The church is wheelchair accessible. Dress modestly and respectfully when visiting. Photography is allowed but avoid flash during services.
